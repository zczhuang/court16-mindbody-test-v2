Turn off reCAPTCHA on the Trial form

A ten-second HubSpot portal toggle that unblocks every API-driven submission the booking app makes.

What
Uncheck "Protect this form from spam with Google reCAPTCHA" on the Court 16 Trial form (3e966ac4-872e-49ec-9b93-1f114fa6d39b) in HubSpot.
Why
The booking app submits to the Forms v3 Integration endpoint server-side. HubSpot currently returns 400 FORM_HAS_RECAPTCHA_ENABLED on every submission because reCAPTCHA is turned on. With reCAPTCHA off, the existing nurture + the new Package A workflows receive submissions as intended.
Who
Ibtissam. Takes under a minute once she's in the HubSpot portal.
Status
Instructions documented · awaiting Ibtissam

Why this is unblocking

The current booking app has a graceful-degradation path: when MindBody is down, the app still submits to HubSpot as status: manual_review so a human can follow up. Today those submissions get rejected by HubSpot's reCAPTCHA check, so the "we'll reach out" response the parent sees corresponds to zero actual lead capture.

Once this toggle flips, every manual_review submission (and every other form-backed flow) lands on the Contact as expected. No code changes needed on the app side.

The steps

  1. Log into HubSpot (app.hubspot.com) as an admin with Marketing permissions.
  2. Marketing → Forms.
  3. Open the Court 16 Trial form. Confirm the form ID in the URL matches 3e966ac4-872e-49ec-9b93-1f114fa6d39b.
  4. Settings tab (top of the form editor).
  5. Scroll to the spam-protection section.
  6. Uncheck "Protect this form from spam with Google reCAPTCHA."
  7. Publish / Update to save.
The form stays live through the change. Embeds on court16.com continue working; they simply stop requiring users to prove they're not robots. If spam becomes a problem in practice, HubSpot's built-in honeypot field and Akismet-style filtering kick in automatically — no action needed.

Verification

  1. After saving, curl the app's /api/book/intro against a sandbox-degraded MindBody setup — expect the trace to show hubspot.submitTrialForm status=ok rather than the 400 error previously.
  2. Open the corresponding Contact in HubSpot → confirm the court16_* custom properties are populated on the Contact.
  3. If anything breaks, re-enable the checkbox — it's a one-click reversal.
